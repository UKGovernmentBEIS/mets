Monitoring Approaches

Most installations generate greenhouse gas emissions only through the combustion of fuel for heat and power. The fuels used are typically commercially available standard fuels.

Installations in this situation are able to produce a monitoring plan using a calculation approach to estimate the amount of carbon emissions based on the amount of fuel used.

For situations where a calculation approach is not possible, the Monitoring and Reporting Regulations (MRR) should be reviewed to learn more about using a monitoring plan based on a measurement approach - where greenhouse gases are measured directly where they are emitted.

If a monitoring plan cannot be created based on either a calculation or measurement approach, then the last resort is a fall-back approach where greenhouse gas emissions are estimated.

Installations which emit nitrous oxide (N20) or Perflourocarbons (PFC) must also include a monitoring plan for these emissions in alongside a plan for their other emissions.
