Compliance with the monitoring and reporting principles

Some principles are aspirational and you may not be able to confirm absolute compliance. Some are reliant on others being met before compliance can be confirmed.

Accuracy

Aircraft operators shall:

  • ensure that emission determination is neither systematically nor knowingly inaccurate
  • identify and reduce any source of inaccuracies as far as possible
  • exercise due diligence to ensure that the calculation and measurement of emissions exhibit the highest achievable accuracy

Completeness

Monitoring and reporting shall be complete cover all flights performed by the aircraft operator that are defined as an aviation activity listed in Schedule 1 of the ETS Order. The emissions data for the flights shall be complete, while avoiding double-counting.

Aircraft operators need to take appropriate measures to prevent any data gaps in the reporting period.

Consistency, comparability and transparency

Monitoring and reporting need to be consistent and comparable over time. For that reason, aircraft operators shall use the same monitoring methodologies and data sets, subject to changes and exemptions approved by the regulator.

Aircraft operators need to obtain, record, compile, analyse and document monitoring data, including assumptions, references, activity data and calculation factors, in a transparent manner that enables the reproduction of the determination of emissions by the verifier and the regulator.

Integrity of methodology

Aircraft operators shall enable reasonable assurance of the integrity of emission data to be reported. They shall determine emissions using the appropriate monitoring methodologies set out in the Regulations.

Reported emission data and related disclosures shall:

  • be free from material misstatement
  • avoid bias in the selection and presentation of information
  • provide a credible and balanced account of an aircraft's emissions

In selecting a monitoring methodology, the improvements from greater accuracy must be balanced against additional costs. Monitoring and reporting of emissions must aim for the highest achievable accuracy, unless this is technically not feasible or incurs unreasonable costs.
