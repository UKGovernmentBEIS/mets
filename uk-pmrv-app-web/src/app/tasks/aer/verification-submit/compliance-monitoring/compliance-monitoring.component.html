Some principles are aspirational and you may not be able to confirm absolute compliance. Some are reliant on others being met before compliance can be confirmed.

Accuracy

Operators shall:

  • ensure that emission determination is neither systematically nor knowingly inaccurate
  • identify and reduce any source of inaccuracies as far as possible
  • exercise due diligence to ensure that the calculation and measurement of emissions exhibit the highest achievable accuracy

Completeness

Monitoring and reporting shall be complete and cover all process and combustion emissions from all emission sources and source streams from regulated activities, and of all greenhouse gases specified in relation to those activities, while avoiding double-counting.

Operators need to take appropriate measures to prevent any data gaps in the reporting period.

Consistency, comparability and transparency

Monitoring and reporting need to be consistent and comparable over time. For that reason, operators shall use the same monitoring methodologies and data sets, subject to changes and exemptions approved by the competent authority.

Operators need to obtain, record, compile, analyse and document monitoring data, including assumptions, references, activity data and calculation factors, in a transparent manner that enables the reproduction of the determination of emissions by the verifier and the competent authority.

Integrity of methodology

Operators shall enable reasonable assurance of the integrity of emission data to be reported. They shall determine emissions using the appropriate monitoring methodologies set out in the Regulations.

Reported emission data and related disclosures shall:

  • be free from material misstatement
  • avoid bias in the selection and presentation of information
  • provide a credible and balanced account of an installation's emissions

In selecting a monitoring methodology, the improvements from greater accuracy must be balanced against additional costs. Monitoring and reporting of emissions must aim for the highest achievable accuracy, unless this is technically not feasible or incurs unreasonable costs.
